How I Got Into Web Development

At university as a first yearn we were taught C++ but most of the time even though it felt like were programming, I didn't see the point of the programs we were building especially how they would apply in the real world so I wanted to build something cool and useful Apps were not popular that time and the browser was the common way to find information so I came across a book in the library about web development. I took the book home, sat at the edge of my bed. And within 30 minutes, I was learning HTML, and for the first time even though HTML is just a "Markup language", I was excited because the was progress.

how I began with the WAMP stack in 2014 and have a handful of failed startups that no one has ever used but I've learnt a lot from that. Most were built on PHP since it was hyped a a lot because Facebook was using it. I one day stumbled upon an article that highlighted why PHP should be avoided, that really brought me to tears but I was in denial I used its frameworks such as lavarel and code Igniter but found the latter to be fun I should say. But still, the fact that their skeletons were PHP, my mind kept looping to that article. I finally swallowed my pride and migrated to Python. Oh was beautiful, simple and effortless. I could even find apps on the Play store and run it on my phone. I tried frameworks like Djanjo and Flask but because I was building simple websites, I opted for Flask.

I realised that most of my websites, the UI/UX was rubbish because I was more of a backend guy and having to sit on the monitor picking colors and fonts was not worth my time, so that's how I discovered bootstrap v3, and it was great because it was even created and used by twitter. For user interactivity I had to learn JavaScript but that took me time, but the second I came to terms with it, I saw how impactful it had on my work and the term "SPA" and "MEAN stack" were being thrown around much in the term world so I tried Angular Js but never really held on, I sticked to backend again. As years went by, I realised how difficult and ineffeciet it was for the frontend and the backend to communicate. I had to use JavaScript, JSON, AJAX, MYSQL, PHP/FLASK/DJANGO just to respond to the client's request. So I browsed for a solution online and the "MEAN stack" came up again but as I was about to jump ship, a mega yatch swooped in in titled the "MERN stack" and I've been happy ever since. And the funny thing is that React Js was built by Facebook.
